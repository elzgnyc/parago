3 · Where each field comes from
  • ● Full product title + url: parsed from the cart line, full untruncated text, linked to https://www.amazon.com/dp/<asin>. Shipped.
  • ● Product image: cart line item img src, absolute URL passed straight through. Shipped.
  • ● Per-item price: unit price (dollars) read off each cart line. Shipped.
  • ● Quantity: integer from the cart line, defaults to 1. Shipped.
  • ● Star rating + review count: not on the cart page. A background fetch of /dp/<asin> via parseProductMeta scrapes rating (0..5) and review count. Best-effort: shipped, and fail-softs to nothing (no meta line rendered) when Amazon returns a robot-check page.

Easy fields are a small cart-parse change already in checkout enrichment. The hard fields are the only network cost, one extra /dp request per ASIN, and they degrade gracefully: the email and approval page just omit the star line when the fetch is blocked.
